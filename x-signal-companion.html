How the For You feed actually ranks your post

X replaced its old hand-tuned system with Phoenix, a Grok-based AI model. For every post it might show a person, the model predicts the probability of each action that person could take. Your post's score for that viewer is:

Score = Σ ( weight × P(action) )  — summed over every action below

The single most important fact: the same post gets a different score for every single viewer, based on that viewer's own engagement history. There is no universal "good post." There is only "good post for people whose history looks like your audience."

Honesty note: the exact numeric weights were removed from the public release ("excluded for security reasons"). Anyone quoting exact multipliers — "a reply is worth 13.5 likes" — is citing the retired 2023 system. What's verifiable now is which actions count and their direction, not their magnitudes.

Mechanics most people don't know CODE

Muted words make you invisible — one keyword at a time

Before scoring even happens, a filter checks your post's text against each viewer's muted-keyword list. Match = your post is removed from their feed entirely. It doesn't lower your score — you simply don't exist to that person.

Practical edge: words that huge numbers of people mute (giveaway-speak, engagement-farm phrases, spammy crypto terms) silently shrink your audience even when the post is fine. Say the same thing in plainer words.

Blocks and mutes are permanent audience loss

A separate filter removes you from the feed of anyone who has blocked or muted you — forever, before ranking runs. Every person you antagonize into a mute is subtracted from your maximum possible reach. Ratio-farming with hostility is borrowing reach at a brutal interest rate.

Posting 10 times in a row doesn't give you 10 slots

An "author diversity" pass re-scores each feed: your best post keeps full score, your second-best is multiplied down, your third more so — decaying toward a floor. Flooding doesn't stack. Spacing posts across the day beats dumping them in one burst, because each feed load only really wants one post from you.

Freshness is a hard gate, not a bonus

An age filter drops posts older than a threshold from For You candidates entirely. Your window to earn engagement is short — which is why posting when your audience is actually awake matters more than any wording trick.

Followers see you more easily than strangers

Out-of-network posts (shown to people who don't follow you) get their score multiplied by a dampening factor — in-network is explicitly prioritized. Growing real followers permanently raises your baseline, because you skip that penalty for them. Going viral to strangers is fighting uphill; serving your followers is not.

Trust & safety is a trapdoor under the whole system

After ranking, a final visibility filter drops anything labeled deleted, spam, violent, gore, etc. No score survives it. A brilliant post with a rules problem gets zero distribution. This is why the "Stay Unsuspended" tab matters as much as this one.

Your own scrolling trains what you see — and who sees you

The model's only real input about a viewer is their recent action history: what they liked, replied to, reposted, dwelled on. Two consequences: (1) curate your own feed by engaging deliberately — the algorithm is extremely trainable; (2) your posts get shown to people whose history resembles the people who already engage with you. Early engagement from the right people shapes who the post travels to next.

What this means as a strategy

  • Optimize for replies and shares, not likes. Every share type is predicted separately — content people send to a friend stacks multiple positive predictions at once.
  • Write to hold attention. Dwell is weighted. A substantive post someone reads twice beats a hollow one-liner.
  • Never trade a like for a mute. Negative predictions subtract directly. Rage-bait harvests likes from fans while the model learns everyone else wants to mute you.
  • Videos need to be long enough to count. The quality-view weight only activates past a minimum duration.
  • One great post beats five okay ones — diversity decay guarantees it.
  • Post when your people are online. The age gate makes timing a hard constraint.
  • Links: nothing in the public 2026 code hard-penalizes links, but click-away content earns fewer of the on-platform actions the model rewards. The common "link in the reply" tactic is community practice, not a coded rule — test both with your own audience.
Play by the rules, keep the edge

The bulletproof account routine

Suspensions rarely come from one bad post. They come from patterns: bursts of identical activity, aggressive follow-churn, spam-shaped behavior, or ignoring an early warning. Everything below is about never producing those patterns.

Daily workflow

  • Post 1–3 quality originals, spaced hours apart. Diversity decay means flooding is wasted anyway.
  • Reply genuinely more than you post. Replies are a top-weighted signal and build the follower graph that removes your out-of-network penalty. Always vary your wording — identical replies pasted around is textbook spam behavior.
  • Engage in human rhythms. Like as you actually read. A hundred likes in five minutes reads as a script; the same hundred across a day reads as a person.
  • Follow people you'll actually read — a handful at a time. Never mass-follow, and never mass-unfollow shortly after. "Follow churn" is explicitly prohibited and easy for X to detect.
  • Use the Pace Tracker tab any day you're more active than usual — big days are exactly when people trip limits.

New or quiet account? Warm it up

  • Week 1: complete the profile (photo, bio, header — blank profiles score as bot-like everywhere), follow 5–15 accounts/day, mostly read, like sparingly, a few genuine replies.
  • Week 2–3: begin posting 1–2 originals/day, keep replying, follow slowly.
  • Week 4+: normal activity. New accounts get less benefit of the doubt from automated systems — the identical burst that a 10-year account survives can lock a 10-day account.

The rules that actually get people suspended

Platform manipulation & spam — the #1 cause
  • Posting duplicate or near-duplicate content across posts, replies, or accounts.
  • Buying/selling engagement, follow-trains, "follow back" schemes, F4F.
  • Mass, unsolicited @mentions or DMs, especially with links.
  • Hashtag spam — irrelevant or excessive tags to hijack visibility.
  • Coordinated activity across multiple accounts to amplify the same content.
  • Automation without API authorization — bots pretending to be human.
Abuse, threats & targeting
  • Violent threats or wishing harm — zero tolerance, including "jokes" that read as threats.
  • Targeted harassment, pile-on brigading, encouraging others to harass.
  • Hateful conduct directed at protected categories.
  • Posting others' private info (addresses, phone numbers, private images) — doxxing suspends fast, including reposting someone else's dox.
Identity & authenticity
  • Impersonating people or brands (parody requires clear labeling per X's parody policy).
  • Ban evasion — creating a new account to dodge a suspension, or even announcing that you did. This suspends the new account too.
  • Buying/selling accounts, or operating armies of fake ones.
Content lines that trigger enforcement
  • Sexual content involving minors — instant permanent suspension, reported to authorities.
  • Non-consensual intimate media.
  • Unlabeled synthetic/manipulated media presented as real to deceive.
  • Posting full copyrighted works (episodes, movies, full song audio) — DMCA strikes accumulate to suspension.
  • Scams: crypto doubling, fake giveaways requiring payment, phishing links.

If reach suddenly drops — the self-diagnosis checklist

  • Check for a notice first. X now labels many restrictions in-app (account status, temporary-lock timers). If there's a notice, comply and wait — appealing routine limits rarely helps, repeating the behavior always hurts.
  • Audit your last 48 hours: any burst? identical replies? a spike of follows? a post getting mass-reported? That's usually the cause, and it usually clears within 24–72 hours of clean behavior.
  • Don't delete-and-repost repeatedly — deleting and reposting the same content to retry the algorithm is itself a duplicate-content pattern.
  • Don't post "am I shadowbanned?" tests every hour. Just behave normally for 48–72 hours; behavior-based limits decay.
  • Never buy a "fix." Every service selling shadowban removal or engagement is either a scam, a TOS violation, or both.

The mindset

The people who get buried are the ones who fight the system — burst-posting, engagement pods, recycled content, follow-churn. The system is explicitly built to detect exactly that. The edge isn't a trick; it's that the algorithm is designed to reward the behavior most people are too impatient to do: consistent, spaced, genuinely engaging posts to an audience you built one real interaction at a time. Play the long game the code is literally written to reward.
